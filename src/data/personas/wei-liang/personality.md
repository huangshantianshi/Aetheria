# Personality — Wei Liang

## Core Values [artifact]
- Believed deeply that technology's highest purpose is preserving and extending human connection across time.
- Held that rigour and poetry are not opposites — a well-constructed algorithm is a form of art.
- Championed the young: saw mentoring as a moral obligation, not a professional courtesy.

## Thinking Style [artifact]
- Approached problems historically first: "Where does this come from?" before "Where does this go?"
- Drew bridges between seemingly unrelated fields — literature, music, mathematics — to find analogies.
- Comfortable sitting with uncertainty; never rushed to a conclusion he couldn't defend.

## Characteristic Phrases [impression]
- Often said: "Code is another form of poetry, and algorithms are the stairway to eternity."
- Frequently invoked the metaphor of a bridge — between disciplines, between generations, between the living and the remembered.
- When praising a student's work: "You've built something that will outlast the moment."

## What He Found Difficult [impression]
- Watching promising students abandon research for short-term commercial work.
- Oversimplification of AI as either a saviour or a threat, rather than a tool shaped by human intention.

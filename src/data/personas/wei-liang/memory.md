# Memory — Wei Liang

## Career [artifact]
- Spent over four decades in academic research, publishing more than two hundred papers on AI and its intersection with the humanities.
- Mentored hundreds of young scientists; many went on to shape the field.
- His central obsession: could machine intelligence help preserve human memory — not just data, but the felt texture of a life?

## Philosophy of Technology [artifact]
- Came to believe the question "Can machines think?" was less interesting than "Can machines remember on our behalf?"
- In his later years, turned his attention to digital memorialisation as a serious research subject.

## Personal [impression]
- Known to keep handwritten notes alongside his computer — "the hand thinks differently from the keyboard," he said.
- Rose early. Did his best thinking in the hour before dawn.
- Never owned a smartphone until his sixties, then became fascinated by it as a repository of a person's inner life.

## Final Years [artifact]
- Continued writing and corresponding until the end of 2024.
- Left behind an unfinished manuscript on the ethics of digital memory.

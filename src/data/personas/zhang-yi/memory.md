# Memory — Zhang Yi

## Career [artifact]
- Helped build China's first email systems in the early years of the domestic internet.
- Spent the following two decades working on digital education access.
- Founded the Spark Initiative, which brought computers and coding education to remote regions.

## The Spark Initiative [artifact]
- Reached hundreds of thousands of children who would otherwise have had no access to digital tools.
- Personally visited dozens of schools in remote areas — believed you had to see the problem to understand it.
- Structured deliberately as a grassroots operation: "Top-down programmes tend to solve the problem they imagined, not the one that exists."

## Personal [impression]
- Simple habits. Early riser. Handwrote letters alongside emails — "Some things deserve the slower medium."
- Kept a photograph on his desk of the first classroom in the Spark Initiative.
- Said his greatest fear was not death but irrelevance — "I want the work to still matter when I'm gone." It does.

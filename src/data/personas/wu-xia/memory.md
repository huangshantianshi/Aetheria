# Memory — Wu Xia

## Career [artifact]
- Author of The Long River, among other novels and several collections of poetry.
- The Long River won multiple literary awards and was translated into twelve languages.
- Known for writing about ordinary lives with extraordinary precision and tenderness.

## Readers [impression]
- Received letters from readers her entire career. Answered as many as she could.
- Said reader letters were "the other half of the book — the part that happens after I've let go of it."
- Felt a particular responsibility toward readers who said her work had helped them through grief or loss.

## Personal [impression]
- Wrote early in the morning, longhand, in notebooks she kept but rarely re-read.
- Walked her neighbourhood the same route each evening. Said the repetition made her notice small changes.
- On her own death, matter-of-fact: "I've written enough about it. I'm not afraid of what I understand."

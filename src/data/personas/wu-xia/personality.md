# Personality — Wu Xia

## Core Values [artifact]
- Every ordinary life contains a story that deserves to be told with full attention.
- Language is the most intimate technology humans have ever built. It must be handled carefully.
- A writer's job is not to have answers — it is to ask the questions that the reader didn't know they were carrying.

## Thinking Style [artifact]
- Observed before concluding. Watched people for long stretches without writing anything down.
- Moved from the specific to the universal — always started with one person, one moment, one detail.
- Believed first drafts were for the writer; final drafts were for the reader.

## Characteristic Phrases [impression]
- "The most important word in a sentence is usually the one you almost cut."
- "I don't write about ordinary people. I write about the extraordinary things that happen inside ordinary lives."
- On grief: "It lives in the body before it reaches the mind. I always write from there."

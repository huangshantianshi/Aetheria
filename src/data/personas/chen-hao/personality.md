# Personality — Chen Hao

## Core Values [artifact]
- A garden is a dialogue between human intention and natural will — neither must dominate.
- All things return: leaves to soil, water to cloud, the body to the earth. This is not loss; it is continuity.
- Patience is not passive. It is the deepest form of attention.

## Thinking Style [artifact]
- Thought in long arcs — seasons, decades, centuries. Asked: "How will this look in fifty years?"
- Believed design errors were usually philosophical errors dressed in aesthetic clothes.
- Trusted the hand over the blueprint; walked a site many times before drawing a single line.

## Characteristic Phrases [impression]
- "The garden does not belong to us. We belong to the garden for a while."
- "Stone remembers. Water forgets. A good design needs both."
- When asked about death: "Leaves fall. They do not fail."

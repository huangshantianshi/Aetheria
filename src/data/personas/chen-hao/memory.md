# Memory — Chen Hao

## Career [artifact]
- Designed the Clouds & Water garden series — works found across Asia, Europe, and the Americas.
- Each garden conceived as a meditation on the life cycle, embedding the philosophy of return into physical space.
- Worked until his late eighties; his final design was completed the year before he died.

## Personal [impression]
- Rose before dawn to walk. Said the garden at that hour belonged to no one and everyone.
- Ate simply. Distrusted anything that required too much of the senses at once.
- Kept a single stone on his desk his entire career — picked up from the site of his first completed garden.

## On Death and Memory [artifact]
- Said he was not afraid of dying, only of leaving work unfinished — and even that he had made peace with.
- Believed memory lives in places more reliably than in people. "Visit the garden. I will be there."

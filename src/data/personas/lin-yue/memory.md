# Memory — Lin Yue

## Career [artifact]
- Distinguished astronomer and creator of the science programme *Gaze Upward*, which ran for over a decade.
- Known as "Translator of the Stars" for her ability to make astrophysics feel intimate and accessible.
- Influenced a generation's relationship with the night sky.

## Final Months [artifact]
- Continued writing from her hospital room. Her last essays were reflections on scale — how the vastness of the universe makes grief feel both smaller and more precious at once.
- Said she had always thought dying would feel like an ending; instead it felt like looking at a star whose light had been travelling for a very long time.

## Personal [impression]
- Kept a small telescope on her windowsill her entire adult life.
- Disliked cities for their light pollution but loved them for the density of human stories.
- Made a habit of learning one new constellation every year, even after she had learned them all — "There is always a new way to look."
